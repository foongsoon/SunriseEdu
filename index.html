What is IGCSE?

IGCSE (International General Certificate of Secondary Education) is one of the world's most popular international qualifications for students aged 14 to 16. It was developed by Cambridge Assessment International Education in 1988 and is now widely adopted in over 140 countries worldwide.

The IGCSE curriculum aims to develop students' knowledge understanding, intellectual inquiry, flexibility, and ability to communicate and write in English. It not only focuses on subject knowledge but also emphasizes the cultivation of critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

Did you know? IGCSE offers more than 70 subjects, including 30 language options, allowing students to choose subjects based on their interests and future goals.

IGCSE qualifications are recognized by universities and employers worldwide, including higher education institutions in the UK, USA, Canada, Australia, and Singapore.

IGCSE Grading System

IGCSE uses the A*-G grading scale, with A* being the highest grade and G the lowest. Some examination boards also use the 9-1 grading system.

Grade Percentage Performance Description
A* 90-100% Outstanding
A 80-89% Excellent
B 70-79% Good
C 60-69% Satisfactory
D 50-59% Average
E 40-49% Pass
F-G Below 40% Fail

Core courses typically target grades C to G, while Extended courses target grades A* to C. This dual-track system ensures students of different ability levels receive appropriate challenges and recognition.

Differences Between IGCSE and GCSE

Although both IGCSE and GCSE are secondary school leaving qualifications, there are some important differences between them:

ğŸ“ Curriculum Content

GCSE follows the UK National Curriculum with more UK-specific content, while IGCSE adopts an international curriculum with a more global perspective.

ğŸ“Š Assessment Methods

GCSE typically includes more coursework and controlled assessments, while IGCSE is primarily based on final exams, though some subjects offer coursework options.

ğŸŒ International Recognition

IGCSE is specifically designed for international students and is more widely recognized globally.
